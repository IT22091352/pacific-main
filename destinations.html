Sigiriya Lion Rock Fortress

Central Province

Sigiriya, also known as the Lion Rock, is an ancient rock fortress located in the northern Matale District near the town of Dambulla in the Central Province, Sri Lanka. It is a site of historical and archaeological significance that is dominated by a massive column of rock nearly 200 metres (660 ft) high.

According to the ancient Sri Lankan chronicle the Culavamsa, this site was selected by King Kashyapa (477 – 495 CE) for his new capital. He built his palace on the top of this rock and decorated its sides with colourful frescoes.

  • The Lion's Paws Entrance
  • The Mirror Wall
  • The Frescoes
  • The Water Gardens
  • The Summit Palace Ruins
  • Hiking / Climbing
  • Photography
  • Historical Tour
  • Bird Watching in Gardens
    • Duration3-4 Hours
    Best Time7:00 AM - 10:00 AM
    Ticket PriceApprox. $30 USD
    DifficultyModerate Climb
    PhotographyAllowed (No Flash on Frescoes)

    Temple of the Tooth

    Kandy

    Kandy, the last capital of the ancient kings of Sri Lanka, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the spiritual heart of the island. Set around a picturesque lake and surrounded by misty mountains, Kandy is home to the sacred Temple of the Tooth Relic.

    The city hosts the famous Esala Perahera festival, one of Asia's most spectacular cultural pageants, featuring decorated elephants, traditional dancers, and fire performers.

    • Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa)
    • Kandy Lake and scenic walking paths
    • Royal Botanical Gardens in Peradeniya
    • Traditional Kandyan dance performances
    • Bahirawakanda Vihara Buddha Statue
    • Cultural Show Visiting
    • Lake Round Walk
    • Botanical Garden Tour
    • Tea Museum Visit
    Duration1-2 Days
    Best TimeDec - Apr, July/Aug
    SignificanceSacred Buddhist Site

    Nuwara Eliya

    Central Province

    Known as "Little England," Nuwara Eliya is Sri Lanka's premier hill station, sitting at 1,868 meters above sea level. The town retains much of its colonial charm with English-style bungalows, manicured gardens, and a cool climate.

    Surrounded by emerald tea plantations, Nuwara Eliya is the heart of Sri Lanka's tea industry, producing some of the world's finest Ceylon tea.

    • Tea plantation tours and factory visits
    • Gregory Lake for boating and picnics
    • Victoria Park botanical gardens
    • Horton Plains National Park and World's End
    • Colonial architecture and golf course
    • High Tea Experience
    • Pony Riding
    • Strawberry Farm Visit
    • Golfing
    Duration2-3 Days
    Best TimeJan - Mar
    ClimateCool (10-20°C)
